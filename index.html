Process

Voting Steps

Complete your voter registration before the local deadline using the official election office portal or an approved in-person form.

Review accepted identification rules for your jurisdiction and make sure your name and current address match your voter record.

Use your assigned polling place or voting center, then check opening hours, transit access, and accessibility services before Election Day.

Mark your ballot carefully, ask a poll worker for a replacement ballot if you make a mistake, and follow posted privacy instructions.

Submit the ballot using the scanner, drop box, or official envelope process, then keep any receipt or tracking reference provided.

Quick answers

FAQ

Update your voter record after a name, address, party, or eligibility change. Deadlines vary by jurisdiction.

Many jurisdictions offer early voting or mail voting. Check your official election office for dates and rules.

Accessible voting equipment, curbside voting, language support, and helper rules are commonly available through election offices.

A provisional ballot preserves your vote while officials verify eligibility, registration, or polling place questions.

Some jurisdictions provide online tracking for mailed or provisional ballots. Use only official government lookup tools.
